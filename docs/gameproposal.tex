\section{Game Overview}

\begin{tcolorbox}[oceansection, title={\faGamepad\quad Game Description}]
\textbf{Ocean Adventure: Grand Tide} is a fast-paced 3D survival game in which the player
takes on the role of a castaway stranded on a drifting wooden raft in the middle of a vast,
dangerous ocean.

\vspace{6pt}

The primary objective is to \textbf{survive as long as possible} by defending the raft against
waves of dynamically spawning enemy marine ships and floating ocean hazards. The player wields
a \textbf{magical fire-based combat system} — aiming and launching projectile attacks to destroy
enemy vessels before they can ram into and destroy the raft.

\vspace{6pt}

As the player's survival score increases, enemy waves grow faster and more numerous, escalating
the tension with every passing moment. The world is brought to life through a \textbf{full
day-and-night cycle} illuminated by dynamic lights, fire-glow from projectiles, and explosion
flares that cast real-time colour onto the surrounding water and raft geometry.

\vspace{6pt}

{\centering
\begin{tabularx}{0.95\linewidth}{@{}l X l X@{}}
    \textcolor{oceanbright}{\faFlag}\;\textbf{Genre:}      & 3D Survival / Arcade  &
    \textcolor{oceanbright}{\faEye}\;\textbf{Camera:}      & Third-Person Dynamic  \\[4pt]
    \textcolor{oceanbright}{\faClock}\;\textbf{Win Goal:}  & Max survival time     &
    \textcolor{oceanbright}{\faBolt}\;\textbf{Core Loop:}  & Aim $\rightarrow$ Shoot $\rightarrow$ Survive \\
\end{tabularx}

Lighting &

\rowcolor{rowalt}
\textcolor{goldaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Dynamic Elements &
Enemy ships \textbf{spawn dynamically} and navigate toward the player's raft. Floating barrel hazards drift across the scene. Every object reacts to player actions. \\

\textcolor{fireaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Interaction \& Game Logic &
Core loop: \textit{aim} with the cursor, \textit{shoot} fire projectiles, \textit{score points} per destroyed ship. Difficulty scales with the survival timer. Clear win/lose conditions are defined. \\

\rowcolor{rowalt}
\textcolor{goldaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Collision Detection &
\textbf{3D bounding-box collision} for ship-raft impacts; \textbf{ray-sphere casting} for projectile-to-ship hit registration. \\

\textcolor{fireaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Camera Motion &
\textbf{Third-person dynamic camera} follows the player, pans with aiming input, and smoothly adjusts height/distance during combat. \\

\rowcolor{rowalt}
\textcolor{goldaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Sky Sphere &
Full \textbf{sky sphere} with a seamless ocean-sky panoramic texture. Blends between day and night sky states as the in-game time progresses. \\

\textcolor{fireaccent}{\faCheckCircle} &
Post-Processing &
Custom \textbf{screen-shake} + \textbf{red-vignette pulse} effect triggered on raft damage. Optionally, a \textbf{chromatic aberration} burst on explosions — both implemented via framebuffer post-processing. \\

\textbf{Menu State} (title screen, start/quit buttons) and \textbf{Play State} (active gameplay), with clean transitions. All scene data loaded from \texttt{.jsonc} config files — \textbf{no hard-coded scene data}. \\

\subsection{Prototype-First Development Strategy}

\begin{tcolorbox}[
    colback=oceandeep!5, colframe=oceanbright!40,
    arc=4pt, boxrule=0.8pt,
    left=8pt, right=8pt, top=6pt, bottom=6pt
]
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt]
    \item \textbf{Minimal Prototype:} Single raft mesh, one enemy ship that moves toward the player, basic fire projectile with ray-sphere hit detection. Verify all Phase~1 rendering systems work together.
    \item \textbf{Core Mechanics:} Add spawn manager (waves), score counter, raft HP, and game-over condition.
    \item \textbf{Polish:} Integrate lit materials, day-night directional light, dynamic point lights from fire/explosions, sky sphere, and post-processing effects.
    \item \textbf{Content Expansion:} Full ocean environment, all 3D models, complete config-file scene descriptions, and menu state integration.
\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}
